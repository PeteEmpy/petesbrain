## 14. Key Learnings: Short vs. Long Time Periods

### What We Learned from This Analysis

**Short time periods (3-7 days) are insufficient because:**
1. **Low statistical power:** Many terms have <10 clicks (not enough for confident decisions)
2. **Seasonal noise:** Day-of-week effects, promotional periods mask true patterns
3. **Conversion lag compounds issues:** 26-66% lag means recent data always incomplete
4. **False positives:** Terms that appear "bad" in 7 days may convert given more time

**Longer time periods (30-60 days) provide:**
1. **Statistical significance:** 30+ click threshold ensures reliable patterns
2. **Pattern capture:** Sufficient time to see true performance trends
3. **Reduced noise:** Seasonal and daily variations average out
4. **Confident decisions:** Lower risk of false positives or premature actions

**Practical example from this analysis:**
- **"led strip lights"** (7 days): 26 clicks, 0 conv ‚Üí "might be bad, but not enough data"
- **"led strip lights"** (60 days): 177 clicks, 0 conv ‚Üí "definitely underperforming, confident negative candidate"

**Conclusion:** For negative keyword decisions, always use 60-90 day lookback with 30+ click minimum threshold.
