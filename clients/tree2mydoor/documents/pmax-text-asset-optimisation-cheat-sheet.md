## üé® CREATIVE PATTERNS IDENTIFIED

### **‚úÖ What Works (Keep Using)**
1. **Brand trust signals:** "Tree2mydoor", "Since 2003", "Established"
2. **Specific products:** Olive Trees, Lemon Trees, Rose Bushes (not generic "gifts")
3. **Emotional benefits:** "Lasting memories", "Grow more meaningful", "Living tribute"
4. **Occasions:** Birthdays, Anniversaries, Memorials (not generic "any occasion")
5. **Urgency + convenience:** "Next day delivery", "Gift wrapped"
6. **Ethical angle:** "Ethical", "Sustainable" (when paired with specific products)
7. **Size/space solutions:** "Small gardens & patios", "Perfect for balconies"

### **‚ùå What Doesn't Work (Avoid)**
1. **Generic superlatives:** "Perfect", "Ultimate", "Fabulous" (without context)
2. **Negative framing:** "Scrap the...", "Why gift X when..."
3. **Feature dumps:** Long lists without emotional benefit
4. **Limiting language:** "Perfect for patio" (without broader appeal)
5. **Vague promises:** "Gifts that last" (too generic)
6. **Clich√©s:** "Gifts that grow & bloom" (overused, meaningless)
