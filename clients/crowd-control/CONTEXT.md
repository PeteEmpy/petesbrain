### **‚≠ê CORE STRATEGY: Product Hero Segmentation Framework**

**CRITICAL FOR AUDIT ANALYSIS**: Crowd Control uses the **Product Hero label system** as its primary campaign segmentation strategy. This is NOT traditional product category segmentation.

**What This Means:**
- Campaigns are structured by **performance labels** (Heroes & Sidekicks, Villains, Zombies), not product types
- Product Hero automatically classifies products based on performance data and syncs labels to Google Merchant Center
- Campaign names "Heroes & Sidekicks", "Villains", and "Zombies" refer to Product Hero performance tiers, not product categories
- This strategy is consistent across Connor Heaps businesses (Grain Guard, Go Glean) and other ROK clients

**Analysis Implications:**
- Budget allocation should consider label-based performance patterns (Heroes typically outperform Villains and Zombies)
- Campaign ROAS targets may differ by label tier (Heroes can sustain higher targets than Villains or Zombies)
- Product movement between labels (Hero ‚Üí Villain ‚Üí Zombie or reverse) will shift campaign performance
- Traditional product category analysis may miss the performance-based segmentation logic
- Frequent ROAS adjustments (especially on Zombies campaign) reflect label performance dynamics, not seasonal changes
