## 5. Key Findings & Implications

### ðŸ”´ CRITICAL CONCERNS

**1. Revenue Per Enrollment Collapse**

The 65.8% drop in revenue per enrollment is the most significant finding. Possible causes:

- **Course Mix Shift:** More students enrolling in lower-priced courses (e.g., short courses vs. diploma programs)
- **Payment Plans:** Increased use of installment plans (revenue recognized over time, not upfront)
- **Discounting:** Heavy promotions to drive volume
- **Student Quality:** Lower-commitment students who pay less upfront

**Action Required:** Urgent analysis of:
- Course enrollment breakdown by price tier
- Payment method distribution (full vs. installment vs. loan)
- Average order value trends by month
- Scholarship/discount usage

---

**2. UK Market Decline**

UK income dropped 15.4% despite 3x enrollment growth:

- Market appears saturated or highly competitive
- May indicate shift to lower-value courses
- Possible conversion tracking issues (revenue attribution)

**Action Required:**
- Review UK campaign targeting and messaging
- Analyze UK course mix changes
- Check conversion tracking accuracy (offline conversions)

---

ROAS dropped from 819% to 538% (-34% efficiency):

- Still profitable (5.4x return) but declining rapidly
- Ad spend increased 61% while income only grew 6%
- Diminishing returns from increased spend

**Action Required:**
- Audit Google Ads campaigns for waste
- Review bidding strategies and ROAS targets
- Consider budget reallocation (reduce less efficient campaigns)

## 6. Root Cause Analysis

### Why Did Revenue Per Enrollment Drop So Dramatically?

**Most Likely Causes (ranked by probability):**

1. **Course Mix Shift** (HIGH probability)
   - More students enrolling in short courses (Â£325-Â£1,250) vs. diplomas (Â£1,645-Â£3,225)
   - Data needed: Enrollment breakdown by course type

2. **Payment Plan Adoption** (HIGH probability)
   - Increased use of installment plans or loans
   - Revenue recognized over time, not as lump sum in current period
   - Data needed: Payment method distribution

3. **Promotional Activity** (MEDIUM probability)
   - Discounts or scholarships to drive volume
   - Data needed: Average discount rate per enrollment

4. **Timing/Attribution Lag** (MEDIUM probability)
   - Students enrolled but haven't paid full course fee yet
   - Installments not yet collected
   - Data needed: Payment collection timeline

5. **Conversion Tracking Issues** (LOW probability)
   - Revenue attribution not matching enrollments
   - Unlikely given data from client's own systems
