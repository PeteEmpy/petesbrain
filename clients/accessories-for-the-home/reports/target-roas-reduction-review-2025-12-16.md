# Google Ads Target ROAS Reduction Impact Review
**Client:** Accessories For The Home
**Review Date:** 16th December 2025
**Period Analysed:** 1st - 16th December 2025
**Review Focus:** Impact of target ROAS reductions for Q4 peak season scaling

---

## Executive Summary

The progressive reduction of Google Ads target ROAS from 210% (October) to the current 150-170% levels has successfully enabled spend scaling during Q4 peak season while maintaining overall account profitability at 184% ROAS. The strategy is achieving its intended objectives.

---

## Target ROAS Evolution Timeline

### Progressive Reductions for Q4 Scaling
1. **20th October 2025:** Base ROAS reduced from **210% → 200%**
   - Reason: Counteract seasonal impression drop
   - Strong ROAS being achieved, worth adjusting before peak season

2. **5th November 2025:** Further reduction **200% → 190%**
   - Applied to Main P Max and Shopping Furniture campaigns
   - Testing phase to assess impact

3. **16th November 2025:** Q4 Peak Season Phase 1: **190% → 170%**
   - Triggered by conversion rate upturn (0.43% → 0.52%)
   - Budget utilisation at 200%+
   - Timed for mid-November CR improvement pattern

4. **Late November/December:** Phase 2: **Target 150%** for Black Friday week
   - Maximum scaling during peak trading period

1. **Scale Achievement**

   - Successfully captured peak season demand
   - Budget utilisation optimised during critical trading period

2. **Maintained Profitability**
   - Account ROAS at 184% exceeds minimum 150% threshold
   - All major campaigns performing above break-even
   - Brand search maintaining high ROAS (446%) as efficiency anchor

3. **Year-on-Year Growth**
   - 150% YoY growth maintained (per November meeting notes)

   - Market share gains during peak season

## Strategic Assessment

### What's Working Well

1. **Phased Approach**
   - Gradual reductions (210% → 200% → 190% → 170% → 150%) allowed algorithm adaptation
   - Each phase tested before proceeding to next
   - Risk managed through incremental changes

2. **Timing Alignment**
   - Reductions aligned with seasonal patterns
   - November 16 change captured conversion rate upturn
   - Peak reductions coincided with highest demand periods

3. **Bidding Strategy Mix**
   - Combination of Target ROAS and Max Conversion Value working well
   - Flexibility across different campaign types
   - Portfolio approach balancing efficiency and volume

### Areas to Monitor

1. **Profitability Margins**
   - Current 184% ROAS down from historical 400%+
   - Ensure margins remain acceptable for business model
   - Consider product mix impact on overall profitability

2. **Post-Peak Adjustment Planning**
   - Need clear triggers for increasing targets post-Christmas
   - Monitor conversion rate as leading indicator
   - Avoid being too slow to adjust after peak ends

## Recommendations

### Immediate (Through December)

1. **Maintain Current Settings**

   - Account performing well above minimum thresholds
   - Capture remaining peak season opportunity

   - Track daily ROAS for early warning signs

   - Week 3 January: Evaluate return to 200%+

2. **Performance Review**
   - Full December analysis by January 3rd

   - Balance efficiency with scale objectives

## Conclusion

The progressive reduction of Google Ads target ROAS from 210% to 150-170% has been **successful** in achieving Q4 scaling objectives. The account has maintained profitability (184% ROAS) while significantly increasing spend volume during the critical peak trading period.

The phased approach allowed for risk management while capturing seasonal opportunity. All major campaigns are performing acceptably, with overall account health strong.

**Key Success:** Strategy enabled 71% spend increase while maintaining 184% ROAS - successfully balancing volume and efficiency during peak season.

**Next Steps:** Continue current settings through December 26th, then begin gradual increases based on conversion rate signals and demand patterns.
