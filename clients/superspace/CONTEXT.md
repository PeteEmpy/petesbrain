## Strategic Context

### Current Strategy

- **Campaign Structure**: Multi-market approach with separate campaigns for US, UK, and Australia
- **Primary Campaign Type**: Performance Max (PMax) with Target ROAS bidding
- **Bidding Strategy**: Target ROAS 500-550% (adjusted seasonally). Q4 2025 plan: Progressive ROAS reduction from 550% to 380% to maximize volume during peak season
- **Key Focus Areas**:
  - Q4 Christmas season optimization (major revenue period)
  - Australia market expansion (launched Aug 2025)
  - Demand Gen campaign testing (launched Sept 2025)
  - Managing stock constraints and budget flexibility
- **Google Accelerator Programme**: Enrolled October 2025 - high-growth potential account with Google assigning additional resources

### Why This Approach?

- **Multi-Country Structure**: US is primary market (90% of sales), with UK and Australia as expansion markets. Separate campaigns allow for market-specific optimization and budget control
- **Target ROAS Strategy**: Product has strong margins (43% gross margin) allowing for aggressive ROAS targets while maintaining profitability. Target CPA goal: ¬£50
- **Seasonal ROAS Adjustment**: Reducing ROAS targets during Q4 to maximize audience building and volume during peak shopping season, accepting lower short-term profitability for long-term customer acquisition
- **PMax Heavy**: Product is highly visual and benefits from cross-channel exposure (Shopping, Display, YouTube, Discovery). Giant magnetic tiles are unique and eye-catching
- **Demand Gen Testing**: Grandparents identified as primary buyers (gift purchases for grandchildren). Demand Gen targeting older demographics (60+) who respond well to video content
- **Stock-Responsive Budgeting**: Physical product with manufacturing lead times requires flexible budget management to avoid over-selling during stock constraints

**Primary Goal**: Revenue growth and customer acquisition during Q4 peak season
**Target ROAS**: 550% (current), planned reduction to 380-450% during Q4 peak (implementation pending - budget increases prioritized first)

## Key Learnings & Insights

### What Works Well

- **Video Content for Demand Gen**: Product is highly visual and experiential. Video ads showing children playing with life-size tiles perform well, especially with 60+ demographic
- **Target ROAS Bidding**: Algorithm performs well with clear profitability targets. Flexible ROAS adjustment strategy allows for volume control
- **Grandparent Targeting**: Discovery that grandparents are primary buyers (not parents) fundamentally changed targeting strategy
- **Multi-Market Approach**: Separate campaigns for US/UK/Australia allows for market-specific optimization and budget flexibility
- **Brand Search Protective Bidding**: Algorithm's conservative approach to "superspace amazon" searches actually protects profitability by avoiding wasteful comparison searches

### What Doesn't Work

- **Portrait Video Only**: Need horizontal format videos for desktop traffic (desktop still significant). FB-focused portrait videos don't cover full placement needs
- **Aggressive Brand Defense**: Attempting to dominate "superspace amazon" brand searches would sacrifice profitability for visibility
- **Static Content for Demand Gen**: Product needs motion/experience to sell - static images underperform vs video
