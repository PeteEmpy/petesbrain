## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors: Boutique PPC Agencies

**Who They Are:**
- 5-20 person agencies
- Regional or specialised focus
- Mix of junior and senior staff
- Traditional service model

**Their Weaknesses:**
- Junior account managers (client complaint)
- High churn (juniors leave, relationships reset)
- Inconsistent quality (depends on who works on your account)
- Limited owner access (busy with sales/management)
- Slower to adopt AI/automation (risk-averse)

### Direct Competitors: Freelance PPC Consultants

**Who They Are:**
- Solo practitioners
- Often part-time or multiple clients each
- Varying levels of expertise
- DIY setup (no infrastructure)

**Their Weaknesses:**
- Limited expertise (variable quality)
- Limited capacity (only one person)
- No infrastructure (no automation, tools)
- No continuity (if they leave/retire)
- Limited business acumen

### Indirect Competitors: Large Holding Companies

**Who They Are:**
- WPP, Publicis, Havas, Zenith, etc.
- Huge teams and global operations
- Full-service offering
- Enterprise focus

**Their Weaknesses:**
- Expensive (£10K-£100K+/month retainers)
- Bureaucracy (slow decisions)
- Generalist approach (not PPC specialists)
- Junior account managers (unlikely to work with director-level)
- Slow adoption of new tools/approaches

### Indirect Competitors: In-House Marketing Teams

**Their Weaknesses:**
- Limited expertise (unless they hired a PPC expert)
- Resource constraints (they wear multiple hats)
- Lack of external perspective
- Limited access to new ideas/tools
- Opportunity cost (time better spent on strategy)

### Indirect Competitors: DIY Tools

**Their Weaknesses:**
- No strategic guidance
- Optimisation is mechanical (not nuanced)
- No business understanding
- No accountability
- Users often make expensive mistakes

## Market Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Google Platform Changes
**Mitigation:**
- Daily monitoring (catch changes early)
- Flexible approach (adapt strategies quickly)
- Knowledge base updated (capture learnings)

**Impact:** Low (we adapt quickly)

---

### Risk 2: AI Commoditisation
**Mitigation:**
- Build moats beyond AI (experience, relationships, context)
- Continuous innovation (stay ahead)
- Tool licensing (monetise our head start)
- Thought leadership (positioning advantage)

**Impact:** Medium (2-3 years before risk materialises)

---

### Risk 3: Solo Capacity Limits
**Mitigation:**
- Automation continues to improve
- Tool licensing creates revenue without clients
- Client pricing can increase (premium positioning)
- Selective growth (quality over quantity)

**Impact:** Low (automation mitigates, licensing is Plan B)

---

### Risk 4: Economic Downturn
**Mitigation:**
- Demonstrate ROI clearly (ROAS improvement)
- Help clients reduce waste (efficiency gains)
- Diversify client base (all verticals, not one)
- Recession = efficiency demand (we're positioned well)

**Impact:** Medium (affects revenue, but positively positioned)
