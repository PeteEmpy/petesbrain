## Summary

- Modern marketing attribution faces growing challenges from privacy constraints and evolving data availability
- Marketers must navigate a paradox of expanding and shrinking data pools simultaneously
- Zero-click searches and privacy regulations are creating new blind spots in attribution models
- Marketing teams face persistent pressure to prove ROI despite deteriorating data visibility
- Attribution strategies need adaptation to account for data gaps in the current marketing landscape

## Key Insights

- Attribution accuracy is declining due to privacy regulations (GDPR, iOS tracking changes) and platform restrictions, requiring marketers to develop new measurement frameworks
- Zero-click searches represent a significant blind spot where user intent and engagement occur without measurable conversion events
- The traditional reliance on last-click or multi-touch attribution models is becoming less reliable, necessitating broader measurement strategies
- Marketing teams should consider implementing modeling solutions, incremental testing, and cross-channel measurement approaches to compensate for data gaps
- Understanding and documenting attribution limitations is as important as the attribution itself for setting realistic expectations with stakeholders
