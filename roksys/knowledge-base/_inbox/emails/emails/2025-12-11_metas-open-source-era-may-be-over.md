# Meta‚Äôs open-source era may be over

View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/7dda0499-a0d8-4d03-8f90-d5a899bb39e2/ART1__1_.jpg?t=1765443567)
Caption: 

After spending billions and enduring significant staff turnover to revamp its AI unit, Meta is weighing a move to make its new model, nicknamed Avocado, closed source, multiple outlets reported.

Since open-source Llama 4 received mixed reviews following its April release, Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg has embarked on an ongoing crusade to reverse the company‚Äôs AI fortunes. Meta aqui-hired Scale CEO, Alexandr Wang, and co. went on an AI hiring spree to build, in Zuckerberg‚Äôs telling, ‚Äúthe most elite and talent-dense team in the industry.‚Äù Wang‚Äôs team, one of a few AI teams within Meta, is known internally as TBD Lab. While pay packages [reportedly stretching up to nine figures](https://www.thedeepview.com/newsletter/zuckerberg-s-1b-bet-on-ai-mercenaries) have attracted talent, the group has reportedly not cohered.

Meta‚Äôs AI restructuring led some researchers to depart for other AI labs. Meta‚Äôs chief AI scientist, Yann LeCun, left in November to found his own startup. CNBC has reported that LeCun was rankled by the 600 layoffs that Meta Superintelligence Labs (MSL) underwent in October. Just this week, MSL employees [Sang Michael Xie](https://x.com/sangmichaelxie/status/1998448143831339510?s=20) and [Vitaliy Chiley](https://x.com/vitaliychiley/status/1998404366202736841?s=20) decamped from the company.

Those still at Meta have reportedly clashed on direction. Senior executives [said](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/12/10/technology/meta-ai-tbd-lab-friction.html?unlocked_article_code=1.7k8.3WNe.Do59teBc6040&smid=url-share#) some of Meta‚Äôs AI efforts should be oriented toward improving the company‚Äôs social media and advertising businesses, while Wang argued Meta should catch up with frontier models offered by OpenAI and Google before focusing on products, per The New York Times. The Times also reported that budget cuts to Meta‚Äôs metaverse team were rerouted to Wang‚Äôs unit.

TBD Lab‚Äôs new frontier model, being closed source, would represent a major strategic shift for Meta, which has long touted its open-source AI efforts. The risks of open-source development became clear when DeepSeek‚Äôs R1 model incorporated components of Llama‚Äôs architecture, which angered some at Meta, CNBC [reported](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/09/meta-avocado-ai-strategy-issues.html).¬†

Open-source cuts both ways, though. TBD Lab is using Alibaba‚Äôs Qwen model as part of the training process for Avocado, Bloomberg [reported](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-12-10/inside-meta-s-pivot-from-open-source-to-money-making-ai-model).

View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/9e7d31c7-673b-475f-9c87-cf6c1be7b363/Frame_1410104594.png?t=1765345175)
Caption: 

Meta has fallen behind other open-source AI models, so it‚Äôs pivoting to the even more competitive field of closed source AI models. Keeping Llama open-source could have created a wedge for Meta to differentiate itself from other hyperscalers, but as things currently stand, it‚Äôll be up to Wang to lead Meta‚Äôs proprietary model past those offered by OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic. In the words of Wang‚Äôs unit within Meta, the odds of that happening are very much TBD.

# Tech firms target AI‚Äôs power problem

View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/59995986-f67b-42a4-9532-2a3ea3069c13/ART2__2_.png?t=1765443573)
Caption: 

AI is doubtlessly causing an energy crisis. Investors are placing their bets on how to solve it.¬†

On Wednesday, [Fervo Energy,](https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/10/google-invests-in-fervos-462m-round-to-unlock-even-more-geothermal-energy/) an enhanced geothermal energy startup, raised $462 million, bringing its valuation to $1.5 billion. Fervo‚Äôs backers include a slew of major investors, climate tech firms and Google, which has a previous partnership with the company to supply its data centers with electricity.¬†

The funding will help Fervo continue its efforts to construct a 500-megawatt power plant in Utah, as well as break ground on other sites.¬†

‚ÄúFolks realize that there‚Äôs an urgency to adding new power generation to the grid, and that the AI race is going on right now,‚Äù Fervo CEO Tim Latimer told the [Wall Street Journal](https://www.wsj.com/finance/fervo-energy-raises-462-million-lands-google-as-investor-27a06c13?st=Tk1GJ2&reflink=desktopwebshare_permalink).¬†

And Fervo isn‚Äôt the only one raking in cash to alleviate the energy problem. Boom Supersonic, a company that builds clean natural gas turbines, announced $300 million in funding on Tuesday.

* The company [said in its press release](https://boomsupersonic.com/press-release/boom-supersonic-to-power-ai-data-centers-with-superpower-natural-gas-turbines-adds-300-million-in-new-funding) that Crusoe, an AI infrastructure firm, will be the launch partner for its 42 megawatt turbine to deliver ‚Äúreliable energy to AI data centers.‚Äù The firm also revealed that it has a backlog of more than $1.25 billion for its turbines.¬†

* ‚ÄúSupersonic technology is an accelerant‚Äîof course for faster flight, but now for artificial intelligence as well,‚Äù CEO and founder Blake Scholl said in a press release.

With AI data centers facing a projected [power shortfall of up to 13 gigawatts by 2028](https://archive.thedeepview.com/p/ai-could-cause-a-power-shortfall), tech firms and investors alike are scrambling for solutions.¬†

These financing rounds are among the many examples of companies funding ambitious ideas to address the problem before it goes from bad to worse. For instance, bets on [fusion and nuclear energy](https://archive.thedeepview.com/p/eu-launches-ai-plan-to-catch-us-and-china) have ramped up, and Meta has even [jumped into energy trading](https://archive.thedeepview.com/p/google-is-making-openai-nervous) as a means of shoring up US power plants.¬†

And some tech giants are even targeting literal moonshots: [Google](https://archive.thedeepview.com/p/google-targets-space-based-ai-infrastructure), [Jeff Bezos‚Äô Blue Origin and Elon Musk‚Äôs SpaceX](https://www.wsj.com/tech/bezos-and-musk-race-to-bring-data-centers-to-space-faa486ee?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqc3QNUhMB29ZsAQ4vkPpzjXLIvJMMiyg09ToFhFAsPNmxuOXokuB1_zkmPn38c%3D&gaa_ts=6939e21e&gaa_sig=OiFtO2ozILJ3IuMKwD2Eyr3V5Vc7qs8r6W7ZYGmCffSKB-VjTN5oOxLzl19DSJLzeF2eaWD4DRckXP_8SWkfmA%3D%3D) are all exploring ways to deploy data centers in space, harnessing the sun‚Äôs energy directly without burdening the already overburdened power grid.

Rather than slow down the breakneck pace of their growth, they‚Äôre seeking the energy they need by any means necessary. However, whether or not the speed of climate innovation can keep pace with AI‚Äôs hypergrowth is still unclear.

# Amazon, Microsoft make big commitments in India 

View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/1ba167ed-4408-4296-92c9-1c4b5c050dd2/ART3__8_.png?t=1765449918)

AI-linked firms are racing to gain a foothold in India.

Amazon [announced](https://www.aboutamazon.com/news/company-news/amazon-35-billion-india-investment) plans to invest $35 billion in India by 2030, with a large portion of the push being driven by AI-enabled selling and shopping. The news came shortly after Microsoft [said](https://apnews.com/article/india-microsoft-ceo-nadella-modi-artificial-intelligence-e1d0f47dea566488236bdd2c9dd577ed) it would invest $17.5 billion to advance India‚Äôs cloud and AI infrastructure.

India is the world‚Äôs most populous nation, contains a relatively skilled and low-cost labor pool, and has not [seen](https://www.technologyreview.com/2025/07/04/1119705/inside-indias-scramble-for-ai-independence/) much success from home-grown AI developers. * In October, Google [announced](https://apnews.com/article/google-artificial-intelligence-visakhapatnam-modi-pichai-ba8fd50e11e41bbdb72097dd10262283) it would invest $15 billion in India over the next five years to construct an AI hub in the southern city of Visakhapatnam.

* Intel will [explore](https://www.theregister.com/2025/12/09/intel_tata_india_semiconductor_alliance/) making chips in India after striking a partnership with the Indian corporation Tata.

* [OpenAI](https://techcrunch.com/2025/08/18/openai-launches-a-sub-5-chatgpt-plan-in-india/) and [Google](https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/10/google-launches-sub-5-ai-plus-plan-in-india-to-compete-with-chatgpt-go/) have both offered discounted subscriptions for Indian users accessing ChatGPT and Gemini.

* Perplexity [offered](https://archive.thedeepview.com/p/perplexity-s-downloads-jump-from-790k-to-6-69m-in-one-month?utm_source=thedeepview&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=umg-forges-ai-partnerships) free subscription trials to customers of the Indian telecom giant Bharti Airtel.

* Anthropic [announced](https://www.anthropic.com/news/expanding-global-operations-to-india?utm_source=thedeepview&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=umg-forges-ai-partnerships) plans to open an office in Bengaluru.
