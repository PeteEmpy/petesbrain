## Summary

- Google Ads has quietly rolled out a new 'Original Conversion Value' metric that shows unadjusted revenue data
- This metric provides transparency by revealing true campaign revenue before Google's attribution modeling and conversion value adjustments
- Advertisers can now compare original conversion values against adjusted values to understand the impact of Google's algorithmic modifications
- The update addresses long-standing advertiser concerns about clarity in conversion value reporting
- This is a significant platform change for conversion tracking and campaign performance measurement

## Key Insights

- The Original Conversion Value metric fills a critical gap in reporting transparency, allowing advertisers to see actual revenue before Google's adjustments are applied
- This enables better reconciliation between Google Ads data and first-party analytics/CRM systems
- Advertisers should audit their conversion value reporting to understand the delta between original and adjusted values
- This metric is essential for accurate ROAS calculations and budget allocation decisions based on true revenue performance
- The quiet rollout suggests Google is responding to advertiser feedback about measurement transparency without making it a major announcement
