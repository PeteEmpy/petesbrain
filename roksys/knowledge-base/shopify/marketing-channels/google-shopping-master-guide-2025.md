## Summary

- Google Shopping is a product advertising platform for retailers to showcase items to active searchers
- Three main steps: Merchant Center account, product feed submission, campaign creation
- Feed refresh required every 30 days minimum (automated solutions recommended)
- Google Shopping accounts for ~65% of all Google Ads clicks for retailers (2025)
- Case study: 27% spend reduction + 71% revenue increase in 60 days through optimization
- Shopify Google & YouTube app enables real-time feed synchronization

## What is Google Shopping?

Google Shopping is an advertising platform that allows businesses to reach people searching for similar products in its search engine. Unlike text ads, Shopping ads display:
- Product image
- Title
- Price
- Store name
- Reviews/ratings (if available)
- Special offers/promotions

## Core Setup Process

### 1. Google Merchant Center Account
- Central hub for managing product data
- Connects to Google Ads for campaign management
- Verifies website ownership
- Monitors product data quality and compliance

### 2. Product Feed Submission
**Essential Product Data**:
- Product titles
- Descriptions
- Prices
- Images (high resolution)
- GTINs (where available)
- Product categories
- Availability status

**Feed Management**: Google requires feeds refreshed every 30 days. Automated systems recommended for:
- Real-time inventory updates
- Price synchronization
- Product availability status
- New product additions

### 3. Campaign Creation in Google Ads
**Configuration Elements**:
- Sales goals (traffic, conversions, revenue)
- Bidding strategy selection
- Geographic targeting parameters
- Budget allocation
- Campaign priority settings

## Bidding Strategies

### Manual CPC Bidding
**Pros**:
- Complete control over bid amounts
- Suitable for small catalogs
- Direct control over spend

**Cons**:
- Becomes difficult at scale
- Time-intensive management
- Requires constant monitoring

**Best for**: Small catalogs (<100 products) or highly specialized products

### Automated Bidding (Recommended)
Leverages machine learning to optimize for specific goals:

**Target CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)**
- Set target cost per conversion
- Google adjusts bids to achieve target
- Best for: Consistent conversion value products

**Target ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)**
- Set target return percentage (e.g., 400%)
- Google optimizes for revenue goals
- Best for: Varied-price catalogs, maximizing revenue

**Maximize Conversions**
- Get maximum conversions within budget
- No specific CPA target required
- Best for: Growing conversion volume

**Maximize Conversion Value**
- Optimize for highest total revenue
- No specific ROAS target required
- Best for: Maximizing overall revenue

**Enhanced CPC (eCPC)**
- Manual bidding with automated adjustments
- Google increases/decreases bids for likely converters
- Best for: Transitioning from manual to automated

## Advanced Optimization Techniques

### 1. Product Title Optimization
**Structure**: Place important information at the beginning (Google reads left to right)

**Example**: "Women's - Tessa 100% Cashmere Turtleneck Sweater"

**Include**:
- Brand name (if recognized)
- Product type
- Key attributes (material, color, size)
- Unique features

**Avoid**:
- Promotional text ("Sale!", "Best Deal!")
- Excessive punctuation (!!!, ???)
- ALL CAPS (except acronyms)
- Keyword stuffing

### 2. Campaign Structure: Address the "Mob Effect"
**Problem**: High-volume, low-converting products consume excessive budget

**Solution**: Restructure campaigns to prioritize profitable products

**Strategies**:
- Separate bestsellers into dedicated campaigns (higher budgets)
- Isolate new products for testing (controlled budgets)
- Create seasonal campaigns for time-sensitive products
- Use priority settings (Low/Medium/High) to control traffic distribution

**Impact**: Case study showed 27% spend reduction + 71% revenue increase

### 3. Negative Keywords: The "Gold Pan Technique"
**Purpose**: Funnel traffic toward high-ROI searches

**Strategy**: Suppress generic, low-intent searches that rarely convert

**Example**:
- Selling leather handbags
- Add negative keywords: "free", "cheap", "DIY", "how to make", "repair"
- Result: 295% higher ROAS for branded searches (client case study)

**Implementation**:
- Start with search terms report
- Identify low-converting queries
- Create negative keyword lists
- Apply at campaign or account level

### 4. Single-Product Ad Groups (SPAGs)
**Use cases**:
- Bestselling items requiring precise bid control
- Smaller catalogs (<500 products)
- High-margin products needing individual optimization
- Seasonal products with varying demand

**Benefits**:
- Granular bid management
- Detailed performance tracking
- Precise budget allocation
- Easier troubleshooting

**Drawback**: Time-intensive for large catalogs

## Merchant Center Advanced Features

### Annotations and Badges
**Automatic displays** (no additional cost):
- Sale prices
- Free shipping eligibility
- Free returns
- Price drops
- Low stock indicators

**Requirements**: Proper feed optimization and Merchant Center configuration

### Merchant Promotions Program
**Available promotions**:
- Percentage discounts (e.g., "20% off")
- Monetary discounts (e.g., "£10 off")
- Free gifts with purchase
- Free shipping offers

**Impact**: 62% of consumers actively search for and use discount codes

**Best practices**:
- Clear promotion terms
- Expiration dates for urgency
- Minimum order requirements
- Combine with seasonal events

### Attribute Rules (Automation)
**Purpose**: Automatically assign values based on existing product data

**Example uses**:
- Tag products with price < compare_at_price as "sale"
- Label products by margin tier (high/medium/low)
- Mark seasonal products automatically
- Flag low stock items

**Benefits**:
- Eliminates manual tagging
- Ensures consistency across feed
- Updates automatically with product changes
- Enables advanced segmentation

## Remarketing Lists for Search Ads (RLSAs)

**Purpose**: Target previous website visitors in Shopping campaigns

**Two modes**:
1. **Observation**: See performance differences, don't change targeting
2. **Targeting**: Show ads ONLY to remarketing audiences

**Strategy**: Bid more aggressively for warm audiences (previous visitors)

**Best practices**:
- Create audience segments by behavior (viewed product, added to cart, purchased)
- Increase bids 20-50% for cart abandoners
- Show specific products users viewed
- Exclude recent converters to avoid wasted spend

## Local Inventory Ads

**Purpose**: Showcase in-store inventory to nearby shoppers

**Features**:
- Display local stock availability
- Show store location and hours
- Enable Buy Online, Pick Up In-Store (BOPIS)
- Drive foot traffic to physical locations

**Requirements**:
- Google Business Profile
- Local inventory feed
- Physical retail locations
- Real-time stock updates

**Best for**: Omnichannel retailers with physical stores

## Key Performance Metrics

**Click-Through Rate (CTR)**:
- Industry average: 0.5-1.5%
- Good: 1.5-3%
- Excellent: 3%+

**Conversion Rate**:
- Industry average: 1-2%
- Good: 2-4%
- Excellent: 4%+

**Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)**:
- Break-even: 100-200% (depends on margins)
- Profitable: 300-500%
- Highly profitable: 500%+

**Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)**:
- Varies widely by industry and product price
- Target: Below average order value × profit margin

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Set it and forget it**: Shopping campaigns require ongoing optimization
2. **Ignoring search terms**: Missing negative keywords wastes budget
3. **Poor product titles**: Generic titles underperform dramatically
4. **Low-quality images**: Poor images kill click-through rates
5. **No segmentation**: All products in one campaign limits optimization
6. **Ignoring mobile**: 60%+ of Shopping traffic is mobile
7. **Not using custom labels**: Missed opportunities for advanced optimization
8. **Outdated feeds**: Disapprovals cause lost revenue
