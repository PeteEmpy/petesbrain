## Summary

- Introduces expected value analysis as a quantitative decision-making framework for evaluating AI product investments and initiatives
- Provides practical methodology for calculating potential returns and risks when prioritizing AI product features and automation projects
- Helps product managers make data-driven decisions by weighing probability of outcomes against their potential impact
- Applicable to evaluating AI/ML model implementations, feature development priorities, and resource allocation decisions
- Bridges the gap between technical AI capabilities and business value assessment

## Key Insights

- Expected value analysis provides a systematic approach to quantifying uncertainty in AI product decisions, enabling more objective prioritization of initiatives
- This framework is particularly valuable for AI/automation strategy decisions where multiple options exist with varying probabilities of success and potential impact
- Product managers can use this methodology to justify AI investments to stakeholders by presenting clear risk-reward calculations
- The approach helps avoid common pitfalls like pursuing high-impact but low-probability AI projects or overlooking moderate-impact high-probability opportunities
