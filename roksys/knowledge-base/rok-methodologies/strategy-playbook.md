## Failed Tests (Avoid Repeating)

### Blanket ROAS Reduction Across All Campaigns

**Client:** Smythson
**Date Proposed:** Oct 28, 2025
**Status:** âŒ NOT IMPLEMENTED (superseded by better strategy)

**Original Plan:**
- Reduce ROAS targets by 15% across ALL Smythson campaigns
- Goal: Increase volume heading into Q4

**Why It Would Have Failed:**
- Ignores regional performance differences (UK ROAS 4.3 vs USA 2.5)
- Blanket approach doesn't account for market maturity
- Risk of unprofitable volume in lower-performing regions
- No strategic differentiation

**Better Alternative (What We Did Instead):**
- Regional ROAS targets based on market performance
- UK: Higher ROAS target (mature market, strong performance)
- USA: Lower ROAS target (growth market, building volume)
- EUR/ROW: Baseline targets for newer markets

**Learning:**
- One-size-fits-all approaches ignore account complexity
- Regional/product-level strategies beat blanket changes
- Always segment by performance before adjusting targets

## Strategy Patterns Library

### When to Split Products into Standalone Campaigns

**Use When:**
- Product revenue >15% of account total
- Distinct seasonality pattern
- ROAS differs >20% from account average
- Need dedicated budget control

**Don't Use When:**
- Product revenue <10% (too small to justify)
- No performance difference from blended average
- Insufficient conversion volume for Google's algorithm

---

### When to Create Category-Specific Asset Groups

**Use When:**
- 5+ distinct product categories
- Categories have different margins/AOV
- Large product catalog
- Want granular performance insights

**Don't Use When:**
- <3 product categories (not worth segmenting)
- Categories too similar in performance/pricing
- Small catalog (<50 products)

---

### When to Use Regional ROAS Targets

**Use When:**
- Operating in 3+ geographic markets
- Clear performance differences between regions (>20% ROAS variance)
- Different market maturity levels (established vs growth)

**Don't Use When:**
- Single market only
- Regional performance is consistent
- Insufficient data per region

## Rollout Prioritization Framework

When a test succeeds, evaluate rollout candidates:

**High Priority (Roll out immediately):**
- Same client, different regions (e.g., Smythson UK â†’ USA)
- Similar business model and product structure
- High confidence of replication

**Medium Priority (Test with modifications):**
- Different client, similar product structure
- May need adaptation for market/catalog differences
- Test on smaller scale first

**Low Priority (Consider for future):**
- Requires significant customization
- Client not ready (budget, capacity, business priorities)
- Uncertain applicability
