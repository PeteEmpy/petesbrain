## Summary

- USPS is adding Delivered Duty Paid (DDP) service to help mailers navigate recent tariff challenges and the end of de minimis exemptions for international shipments
- The new service addresses compliance issues arising from foreign-country tariffs on outbound international items
- DDP will allow merchants to prepay duties and taxes, improving customer experience by eliminating surprise fees at delivery
- This change directly impacts cross-border ecommerce operations, particularly for Shopify merchants selling internationally

## Key Insights

- The end of de minimis exemptions represents a significant shift in international ecommerce logistics that requires merchants to adapt their shipping strategies
- DDP services transfer the burden of customs duties from customers to merchants, potentially improving conversion rates by providing transparent all-in pricing at checkout
- Ecommerce businesses should evaluate whether absorbing or transparently passing through duties and taxes aligns with their pricing strategy and target markets
- This USPS service addition creates parity with private carriers and may offer cost-effective alternatives for smaller merchants handling international shipments
