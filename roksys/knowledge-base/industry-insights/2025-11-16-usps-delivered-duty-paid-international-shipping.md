## Summary

- USPS is launching Delivered Duty Paid (DDP) service in response to increasing foreign tariffs and elimination of de minimis thresholds
- The new service addresses challenges faced by international shippers dealing with customs duties and taxes on outbound shipments
- DDP allows merchants to prepay duties and taxes, providing transparency and improved customer experience for international orders
- This change is particularly relevant for Shopify merchants selling to international markets affected by tariff changes
- The service represents a shift in how USPS handles international shipping logistics and customs processing

## Key Insights

- E-commerce merchants need to adapt fulfillment strategies as de minimis exemptions are eliminated and tariffs increase globally
- DDP services shift the cost burden upfront but improve delivery reliability and customer satisfaction by eliminating surprise fees
- Shopify merchants should evaluate their international shipping strategy and consider carriers offering DDP options to remain competitive
- This regulatory shift may impact pricing strategies for international orders and require updated checkout communication about landed costs
