## Summary

- Creative fatigue occurs when ad audiences become oversaturated with the same creative assets, leading to declining CTRs, rising CPCs, and reduced campaign performance
- Key warning signals include falling click-through rates, increasing cost-per-click, shrinking reach, and declining engagement metrics over time
- Early detection through consistent monitoring of performance trends allows advertisers to refresh creative before significant performance degradation occurs
- Data-driven creative rotation strategies and systematic testing help extend ad lifespan and maintain campaign efficiency
- While primarily focused on social advertising, these principles apply to Google Ads Display, Discovery, and Performance Max campaigns where visual creative plays a central role

## Key Insights

- Creative fatigue is a predictable lifecycle issue rather than a sudden failure - proactive monitoring of CTR trends, frequency metrics, and CPC increases enables early intervention before major performance drops
- Establishing baseline performance metrics and setting alert thresholds (e.g., 20% CTR decline, frequency above 3-4 for social) creates systematic early-warning systems for creative refresh needs
- Data-driven creative rotation schedules based on historical fatigue patterns for specific audience segments help optimize creative lifespan and prevent waste from prematurely retiring effective assets
- While the article focuses on social platforms, Google Ads practitioners should apply these monitoring principles to Display, Discovery, Demand Gen, and Performance Max campaigns where creative assets significantly impact performance
