# AI has a copyright problem

View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/e4b088e1-1ad8-477a-b702-154e0f72926a/ART1__0-00-00-00___37_.jpg?t=1761040930)
Caption: 

Copyright and intellectual property remain major hurdles in embedding AI in creative spaces. Adobe might be looking to fix it.¬†

The company on [Monday launched AI Foundry,](https://www.zdnet.com/article/adobe-mightve-just-solved-one-of-generative-ais-biggest-legal-risks/) a program that works with enterprises to build custom AI models trained specifically on their branding and IP. The models are capable of producing text, images, video, 3D renderings and more.¬†

Adobe launched Firefly, its family of image models, in 2023, with the models‚Äô biggest selling point being that they are trained on licensed data and safe for commercial use.¬†

With Foundry, offering the ability to fine-tune these models stands to make Firefly‚Äôs offering a lot more useful. Hannah Elsakr, VP of generative AI new business ventures at Adobe, [told TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/20/adobe-launches-a-foundry-service-that-builds-custom-generative-ai-models-for-enterprises/) that the Foundry offering is ‚Äúelevating a lot of the capabilities we already had.‚Äù

Adobe‚Äôs offering seeks to reckon with the massive copyright issue plaguing developers of creative AI and those seeking to use it. Several model developers have already faced legal run-ins as a result, with major firms like Anthropic, OpenAI, Meta and Perplexity among them.

An offering like Foundry may also particularly strike a chord as AI video tools like OpenAI‚Äôs Sora, Google Veo, and xAI Imagine gain rapid popularity ‚Äì and [face backlash from the entertainment industry](https://archive.thedeepview.com/p/ai-video-tools-stoke-hollywood-ire) due to the copyright complications at stake.

View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/1434e41d-1774-47dc-9d7d-03db740a83f6/image.png?t=1753043333)
Caption: 

Copyright might not be the only problem that crops up with AI in creative spaces. Adobe is widely known as an industry standard for things like photo and video editing, and its dedication to AI might signal where the creative and entertainment industry are headed. However, a lot of people within creative industries have [ethical qualms about AI‚Äôs use to create art generally](https://www.bbc.com/future/article/20241018-ai-art-the-end-of-creativity-or-a-new-movement). While the pitch for AI Foundry as it stands is targeted towards enterprises, it represents a push to embed generative AI into a process that has long been essentially human: the creation of art.

# Anthropic‚Äôs takes on drug discovery with Claude

View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/b6dc9801-29ac-4547-9715-044c21381c90/ART2__0-00-00-00___38_.jpg?t=1761040955)
Caption: 

Anthropic is going all-in on the $2 trillion biotech market.

On Monday, the AI safety and research startup launched [Claude for Life Sciences](https://www.anthropic.com/webinars/claude-for-life-sciences), a new capability designed to support scientists working on drug discovery: one of the most complex and costly processes in research, [CNBC reported](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/10/20/anthropic-claude-life-sciences-research-ai.html).

Part of the Claude model family, the tool is built to automate time-consuming tasks like hypothesis generation, literature review and regulatory drafting. The goal is to reduce the busywork that slows down early-stage research and development and, in turn, ship potentially life saving treatments to market faster.¬†

It‚Äôs a timely, and arguably Anthropic‚Äôs most practical use of AI yet. Developing a single drug can take [10 to 15 years](https://www.phrma.org/policy-issues/research-development) and cost $2.6 billion. Claude isn‚Äôt running lab experiments or clinical trials, but with its new feature, Anthropic believes it can streamline the paperwork and data handling that often bog down progress.

‚ÄúWe want a meaningful percentage of all life-science work in the world to run on Claude,‚Äù Eric Kauderer-Abrams, head of Anthropic‚Äôs biology and life sciences division, told CNBC.¬†

The launch comes two months after Anthropic [hired Kauderer-Abrams](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/eric-kauderer-abrams-0ba5864a_im-happy-to-share-that-im-starting-a-new-activity-7361093436692676609-dNzz?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_ios&rcm=ACoAACOnMKwBb8Ns32g1U_QqZTfDumyywf9kN6c) to lead its biology and life science division. Since then, Anthropic has partnered with platforms like Benchling, which manages lab data for over 200,000 researchers, and 10x Genomics, known for its genomic sequencing tools, to integrate their tools into Claude.¬†

In a demo shared with _CNBC_, a scientist used Claude to query experimental data in Benchling, generate summary tables, and compile a report ‚Äî cutting a multi-day process down to minutes.

Anthropic isn‚Äôt alone in targeting the drug discovery space. Last week, Google DeepMind released [Cell2Sentence-Scale 27B](https://archive.thedeepview.com/p/why-apple-needs-to-fight-ai-brain-drain?utm_source=thedeepview&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=why-apple-needs-to-fight-ai-brain-drain&_bhlid=aa8626e4885f71c76d70d67a2b923cbec1cb3d0f), a model developed with Yale to help uncover cancer cell behaviors. OpenAI, as well as startups like Recursion and Cradle, are also using AI to analyze proteins and extract insights from lab data.

Kauderer-Abrams is under no illusions that Claude will solve biology‚Äôs hardest problems overnight. But he sees it as a tool that could give scientists a faster path to the next big breakthrough.

# DeepSeek OCR could make AI cheaper, more efficient

View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/c3ff0019-991e-4171-b79b-f9775751b623/ART3__0-00-29-21_.jpg?t=1761040980)
Caption: 

DeepSeek is giving its models more to do.¬†

The Chinese AI firm has developed a new[ OCR, or optical character recognition system](https://the-decoder.com/deepseeks-ocr-system-compresses-image-based-text-so-ai-can-handle-much-longer-documents/), that‚Äôs capable of handling a lot more text with far fewer resources, according to [a paper published by DeepSeek researchers](https://github.com/deepseek-ai/DeepSeek-OCR/blob/main/DeepSeek_OCR_paper.pdf) this weekend. Both the code and the trained model of this proof-of-concept are currently publicly available.

DeepSeek‚Äôs model parses text as images, requiring less computing power than typical models that only handle digital text. The OCR is capable of handling much longer documents without running into memory limits, achieving more than 96% precision in keeping the original meaning and detail of the text when that text is compressed by up to 10 times.¬†

‚ÄúA single image containing document text can represent rich information using substantially fewer tokens than the equivalent digital text,‚Äù the researchers noted. The paper notes that vision encoders can potentially enhance large language models‚Äô efficiency in processing text.¬†

The model was trained on 30 million pages of PDFs. DeepSeek‚Äôs system can handle several kinds of documents, including diagrams and formulas, and supports 100 languages, and can process data extremely quickly. With just one GPU, the OCR can process 200,000 pages per day. A whole cluster, meanwhile, can handle up to 33 million documents a day.¬†

DeepSeek‚Äôs discovery matters for a few reasons. For one, it tackles the problem of context windows, or the amount of information that a model can process at once, [Bob Rogers](https://www.linkedin.com/in/bob-rogers-0a00441/), chief product and technology officer of [Oii.ai](https://Oii.ai) and co-founder of BeeKeeper AI, told The Deep View. The larger the context window, the more expensive the query. DeepSeek‚Äôs system reduces the size of that context window without reducing the amount of information processed.¬†

Additionally, the fact that this tech is open source is ‚Äújust a great bonus,‚Äù Rogers noted. It signals another discovery in developing more cost-effective AI for the Chinese open source company, [which sits as a competitor](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/what-is-deepseek-ai-china-stock-nvidia-nvda-asml/) to AI giants like Google, OpenAI and Anthropic.

‚ÄúIt‚Äôs helping us stay out of AI services prison,‚Äù said Rogers. ‚ÄúWe're starting to use these AI services, and we're getting pretty dependent on them. Having the ability to do real things at scale with more locally deployable models is actually like a pressure release valve for that.‚Äù
